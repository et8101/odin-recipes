Thai Peanut Sauce Recipe

Why I love Thai Peanut Sauce

A good Thai peanut sauce is a culinary secret weapon—versatile, deeply flavorful, and surprisingly easy to make. This isn't just a simple sauce; it's a perfect harmony of sweet, salty, savory, and a hint of spice. While store-bought versions often fall flat, making it from scratch takes only minutes and delivers a richness that can elevate a simple meal into something truly extraordinary. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, this recipe will quickly become a go-to for its simplicity and bold, satisfying taste.

The magic of this sauce lies in its adaptability. It can be thinned out to become a light and zesty dressing for a crunchy salad, used as a rich, nutty glaze for grilled chicken or tofu, or tossed with your favorite noodles for a quick and satisfying dinner. With just a few pantry staples, you can create a sauce that brings the vibrant flavors of Thai cuisine right to your kitchen. So, get ready to discover how a simple blend of ingredients can transform your cooking in the most delicious way possible.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup creamy peanut butter
  • ¼ cup warm water
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for a gluten-free option)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • ¼ to ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes, or to taste
  • Optional: A splash of coconut milk for a creamier consistency

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, maple syrup, toasted sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and red pepper flakes.
  2. Whisk the ingredients together until they form a thick paste.
  3. Slowly add the warm water, about a tablespoon at a time, while continuously whisking. Continue to add water until the sauce reaches your desired consistency. For a thicker sauce for noodles, use less water; for a thinner dressing, add more.
  4. If you desire a creamier and richer flavor, whisk in a tablespoon or two of coconut milk.
  5. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasonings as needed. You can add more soy sauce for saltiness, lime juice for tang, or maple syrup for sweetness.
